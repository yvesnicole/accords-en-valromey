Accords en Valromey was born from cellist Célia Garetti-Nicole's dream of bringing classical music back to her home region of Champagne-en-Valromey. After studying at the conservatories of Paris, Brussels, and Mannheim, she founded the festival in 2022 with Véronique and Hervé to create concerts that are artistically ambitious, welcoming, and deeply rooted in the Valromey.

The association grew thanks to volunteers, musicians, and local residents committed to the cultural life of the valley. Alain, the stage manager, and Michel, the association's secretary, help shape a collective project that gathers outstanding young artists in Champagne church, then around post-concert cocktails where audiences and musicians can meet.

A central mission of the festival is to rediscover overlooked women composers such as Lili Boulanger, Rebecca Clarke, and Alice Lesur. This spirit of transmission, curiosity, and closeness makes Accords en Valromey an intimate, community-driven festival true to the character of the Valromey.
